What's up. Here are some things about me!

My name is Jiayuan Wang, I prefer Jay. I was born near Shanghai, China on February 5, 1992. I moved to Germany when I was 5 years old and lived in Hanover and Berlin for two years. I attended 1st grade in Hanover and 2nd grade in Berlin. I did well in classes, to the teachers' surprise, and picked upspeaking German in a month, and reading and writing in three. When November 2000 came around, I moved to Boston, Massachusetts and was placed in 3rd grade at the Lucy Stone School in Dorchester. I picked up English after 2 months and got the highest overall achievement award for my grade every year, which I was really proud of as a child. I made many friends at my elementary school and still remember their names and what they taught me to help me adapt to living in Dorchester.

For 6th grade I went to the Richard J. Murphy School (also in Dorchester), where I first made friends with white kids; my elementary school was mostly African American and Hispanic. I learned swimming in that middle school and also started playing basketball, my favorite sport. It's something I can't do without even to this day. For 7th till 12th grade, I enrolled in the first high school that was created in the United States back in the colonial days. Boston Latin School, a public exam school known for rigorously preparing its students for graduation and college. Although there were some hard times in my six years at BLS, I have a lot of good memories of the school and now loved my time there, especially my four years of high school.

Right now, I am currently a student of Tufts University, Class of 2014. Tufts is home of the Jumbos, our elephant mascot. My major is in Computer Science of the Engineering school. I love the hilly Medford campus, the friendly students, and the passionate debates that define Tufts and who we are. I've seen a lot of wonderful things and gained a plethora of knowledge in my 21 years, and I look forward to more amazing experiences in the future...
